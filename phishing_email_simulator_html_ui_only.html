How to use

Click Show suspicious highlights to outline elements that are commonly used in phishing emails (spoofed sender, suspicious links, urgent language). Click Show safe tips to add safe markers to the legitimate email.

Checklist (quick)
  1. Check the sender's email closely
  2. Hover before you click — inspect the link
  3. Watch for urgent or threatening language
  4. Look for typos and poor grammar

Safe vs Unsafe — Quick Comparison

  1. Unsafe Misspelled or spoofed sender domain (e.g., amazn-security.com) — Safe Verified domain with consistent reply-to and return-path.
  2. Unsafe Links that point to different domains or long redirectors — Safe Links point to the official domain; use order history for tracking instead of in-email link.
  3. Unsafe Urgency, threats, or countdown timers — Safe Neutral, informative language and contact info.
  4. Unsafe Generic greetings ("Dear customer") — Safe Personalized greetings with your name.
  5. Unsafe Request for credentials or sensitive info — Safe Legitimate companies never ask for passwords via email.
